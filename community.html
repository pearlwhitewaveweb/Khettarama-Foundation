Community Programs

Uplifting Communities Through Compassion and Action

At Khettarama Foundation, we are dedicated to eradicating poverty and empowering underprivileged communities, including children, the Ahikuntika people, and other marginalized groups. Through our outreach programs, we aim to provide education, basic necessities, and opportunities for a better future.

Our programs are designed to address the root causes of poverty by offering support in education, skill-building, and access to essential resources. From scholarships for children to vocational training for adults, we work tirelessly to bring hope and opportunities to those who need them most.

Empowering the Ahikuntika People

The Ahikuntika people, a marginalized community in Sri Lanka, face unique challenges. Our initiatives aim to preserve their cultural identity while helping them integrate into modern society. By providing access to education, healthcare, and sustainable livelihoods, we empower them to thrive without losing their heritage.

We believe in a future where every individual, regardless of background, has the tools and support to lead a dignified life. Join us in making this vision a reality.

Most Venerable Kovane Palitha Thero

Our Honorable Director and spiritual guide, Most Venerable Kovane Palitha Thero, has dedicated his life to enriching communities through mindfulness, cultural preservation, and education. With decades of service as the Chief Incumbent and Deputy Head of Pirivena, his vision has brought countless individuals closer to harmony and understanding.

Through his leadership, our community programs foster values of compassion, cultural respect, and collective growth. Join us in creating a lasting impact.

Get Involved

Japan-Sri Lanka Volunteer Program

The Japan-Sri Lanka Volunteer Program is an initiative designed to foster cultural exchange and mutual support between our two nations. Volunteers from Japan collaborate with local communities in Sri Lanka to promote education, health, and sustainability.

This program offers opportunities for volunteers to teach English, share knowledge of modern agricultural practices, and participate in community development projects. In return, volunteers gain a deeper understanding of Sri Lanka's rich cultural heritage and traditions.

Sri Lanka-Andra Community for Ravana Exploration

In collaboration with our counterparts from Andra Pradesh, India, the Sri Lanka-Andra Community for Ravana Exploration delves into the historical and cultural narratives of the legendary King Ravana. This joint effort aims to uncover archaeological and literary evidence that connects the histories of both regions.

Through cultural research, storytelling workshops, and academic collaboration, this program seeks to shine a light on the shared heritage and create a platform for cultural diplomacy. Participants can engage in exploration tours, cultural exhibitions, and historical research.
